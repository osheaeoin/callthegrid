    How to play
    Call The Grid
    No experience needed. If you watch F1, you already know enough to play. Here's everything explained simply.
    The Basics

    Call The Grid is a free prediction game for Formula 1 fans. Before each race weekend, you predict who you think will finish where — and you earn points based on how accurate you are. It's you and your mates, competing across a full season. No money, no complicated rules — just F1 knowledge and a bit of luck.

    Not at all. If you watch the races and recognise the drivers' names, you have everything you need. More casual fans often do surprisingly well — sometimes a lucky guess beats years of expert knowledge. That's what makes it fun.

    Yes, completely free. There's no subscription, no premium tier, no ads. It's built to be played with friends, not to make money.

    Making Your Picks

    Each race you make six predictions:

    • 🏎️ Pole Position — Who gets the fastest lap in qualifying (P1)
    • P2 Qualifying — Who qualifies second
    • P3 Qualifying — Who qualifies third
    • 🏁 Top 10 Race Finishers — Drag them into your predicted finishing order
    • ⚡ Fastest Lap — Who sets the single fastest lap during the race
    • 💀 First Retirement — Who is the first driver to drop out of the race (DNF)

    You can also play your 🃏 Joker to double one driver's points — more on that below.

    Your picks lock at the start of qualifying — not the race. This is important! Qualifying usually happens on Saturday. Once qualifying begins, you can no longer change your picks. The app shows you a live countdown so you always know how long you have left.

    ⚠️ Don't leave it too late. Get your picks in before Saturday morning to be safe.

    You'll see a list of 10 drivers. Simply drag and drop them into the order you think they'll finish the race. On mobile, press and hold a driver, then drag them up or down. On desktop, click and drag. The position numbers update automatically as you move drivers around.

    You don't need to get all 10 right — every correct driver earns you points, and an exact position earns you more.

    Yes — right up until qualifying starts. After you lock your picks, you'll see an Edit Picks button on the confirmation screen. You can change as many times as you like before the deadline. Once qualifying begins, they're locked for good.

    Scoring
    🎯Driver finishes in your exact predicted position+10 pts
    📍Driver is in your Top 10 but wrong position+5 pts
    🏎️Correct Pole Position pick+5 pts
    2️⃣Correct P2 Qualifying pick+3 pts
    3️⃣Correct P3 Qualifying pick+1 pt
    ⚡Correct Fastest Lap pick+5 pts
    💀Correct First Retirement pick+5 pts
    🃏Joker Wildcard — doubles that driver's race points×2

    Example: You pick Verstappen P1 and he wins → +10 pts. You also picked him as your Joker → +20 pts total just for that one pick.

    If a driver you picked in your Top 10 retires during the race, they simply score 0 points for that pick — they don't count as being "in the top 10". No penalty, you just miss out on those points. This is also why the First Retirement pick is its own separate question worth +5 pts — turning bad luck into a points opportunity.

    Scores update automatically after the race using live results from the F1 API. Usually within a few minutes of the chequered flag. You'll be able to see the full leaderboard breakdown showing who got what right — and who had the most embarrassing picks 😄

    🃏 Joker Wildcard

    The Joker is your secret weapon. Each season you get 3 Jokers to use across 24 races — use them wisely. When you play a Joker on a driver, whatever race points they earn get doubled.

    For example: if you Joker Norris and he finishes P1 (exact finish = +10 pts), you get +20 pts instead. If he also sets Fastest Lap (+5 pts), that doubles too for a total of +30 pts from one driver.

    ⚠️ The risk: if your Joker driver crashes out, you've wasted one of your 3 seasonal wildcards.

    There's no right answer — that's what makes it fun. Some strategies:

    • Street circuits (Monaco, Baku, Singapore) — high chance of safety cars and DNFs, but if your driver survives, big points
    • Dominant driver at their home race — Verstappen in the Netherlands, Leclerc in Monaco
    • Late season — save it for when you need a big swing to catch the leader
    • Favourite driver — sometimes you just back who you love 🤷
    Groups & Friends

    Create a private group in the Groups tab. You'll get a short invite code — share it with your mates. They sign up, enter the code, and you're all in the same group. You'll see a private leaderboard showing just your group's standings alongside the global one.

    One person in your group should create it and share the code via WhatsApp or wherever you already chat.

    Yes. You might have one group for work colleagues and another for your close friends. Your picks are the same for both — you don't pick separately for each group. The groups just give you different leaderboards to compare against.

    After each race, the Standings page shows a special badge for whoever had the most catastrophically wrong prediction that weekend. It's entirely for bragging rights (well, shame rights). There's no points penalty — just eternal group chat humiliation. The algorithm looks at picks that were the furthest from the actual result — like predicting someone to finish P1 when they finished P19 or retired.
